M&M's

M&M's its one of the most famous and beloved candies in the world. These chocolates comes as colorful, button-shaped, coated with crispy candy shell, they come in a variety of flavors, making them a favorite treat for all ages.

Did you know that the slogan for M&M’s is “Melts in your mouth, not in your hands.”

A description about the M&M's

M&M's are small chocolates coated in colorful colors that its made by Mars, Incorporated. They come in a variety of flavors and fillings, such as milk chocolate, peanut, peanut butter, and caramel with each candy having a hard, sugary shell with a printed "M" on it. M&M’s are popular due to their wide variety of flavors, vibrant colors, and fun branding. Their crunchy candy shell prevents melting, making them a convenient, mess-free snack. The brand's iconic spokescandies, each with distinct personalities, add an element of entertainment that appeals to both kids and adults.

Who invented the M&M candies?

M&M’s were created by Forrest Mars Sr., the son of Mars Company founder Frank C. Mars, in 1941. He developed the idea after seeing soldiers in the Spanish Civil War eating chocolate coated with a hard sugar shell to prevent melting. Mars partnered with Bruce Murrie, the son of Hershey’s president, to secure a steady supply of chocolate, which is why the candy was named M&M’s (Mars & Murrie). Initially, M&M’s were sold exclusively to the U.S. military during World War II before becoming widely available to the public.
